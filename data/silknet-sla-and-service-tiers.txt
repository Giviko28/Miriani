SILKNET LLC — SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA) & CLIENT TIER REFERENCE
Internal Operations Document | Rev 5.1 | January 2025

This document is the authoritative reference for account managers, NOC engineers,
and customer success teams when discussing SLA commitments with clients or calculating
service credits after an incident.

SERVICE TIERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TIER 1 — RESIDENTIAL (Bronze)
  Target uptime:        99.5 % per calendar month (~3.6 h allowed downtime)
  Restoration target:   4 hours from ticket creation
  SLA credit:           None (residential is best-effort)
  Support channel:      Call centre: 032 222 8000; self-service portal
  Examples:             Home fiber, residential IPTV, consumer mobile

TIER 2 — SME BUSINESS (Silver)
  Target uptime:        99.9 % per calendar month (~43 min allowed downtime)
  Restoration target:   2 hours from ticket creation
  SLA credit:           5 % of monthly invoice per hour of breach beyond target,
                        capped at 30 % of that month's invoice
  Support channel:      Dedicated SME helpdesk: 032 222 7000 (08:00–22:00)
  Eligible companies:   Annual contract value < 24,000 GEL
  Examples:             Retail shop fiber, small office internet, SME hosted email

TIER 3 — ENTERPRISE (Gold)
  Target uptime:        99.99 % per calendar month (~4.3 min allowed downtime)
  Restoration target:   30 minutes from ticket creation
  SLA credit:           10 % of monthly invoice per hour of breach, capped at 50 %
  Support channel:      24/7 NOC direct line: 032 222 6000; dedicated TAM assigned
  Eligible companies:   Annual contract value 24,000–200,000 GEL
  Examples:             Bank of Georgia SME branches, Silknet B2B dedicated fiber,
                        multi-site MPLS, Tbilisi City Hall connectivity

TIER 4 — STRATEGIC / PLATINUM
  Target uptime:        99.99 % with redundant physical path guarantee
  Restoration target:   15 minutes; automatic failover to backup circuit within 60 sec
  SLA credit:           20 % of monthly invoice per hour of breach, capped at 100 %;
                        persistent breaches trigger contract review clause
  Support channel:      24/7 NOC; named Senior TAM; direct CTO escalation path
  Eligible companies:   Annual contract value > 200,000 GEL or strategic designation
  Current Platinum clients (as of Q1 2025):
    - TBC Bank (all branches + DC interconnect)
    - Bank of Georgia (headquarter + 12 regional branches)
    - Georgian Railways (Tbilisi control centre)
    - Tegeta Motors (head office + 8 showrooms)
    - Cartu Bank (headquarters + Batumi branch)
    - Ministry of Finance of Georgia (primary + DR site)

SLA CREDIT CALCULATION — WORKED EXAMPLE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Client: Cartu Bank (Platinum)
Monthly invoice: 12,400 GEL
Incident: Headquarters fiber down for 2 hours 45 minutes (165 minutes)
Allowed downtime: 4.3 minutes
Breach duration: 160.7 minutes ≈ 2.68 hours

Credit = 20 % × 12,400 × 2.68 = 6,646.40 GEL
Capped at 100 % of monthly invoice → 12,400 GEL would apply if breach exceeded 5 hours.
Actual credit: 6,646.40 GEL applied to next month's invoice.

Process: NOC engineer raises credit request in Jira (project: BILLING) linking the
incident ticket. Finance team approves and applies credit within 3 working days.

PLANNED MAINTENANCE WINDOWS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Standard window: Tuesday and Wednesday nights, 02:00–06:00 Tbilisi Time.
Notice period:
  Residential / SME:  5 business days via SMS + status page
  Enterprise:         10 business days via email to primary technical contact + TAM call
  Platinum:           15 business days; written confirmation required from client

Emergency maintenance (network safety risk only):
  Residential:        2 hours notice via SMS
  Enterprise/Platinum: Phone call to primary NOC contact; simultaneous Jira update

Maintenance carried out inside the agreed window does NOT count against SLA uptime.
Maintenance outside the window (without written client approval) counts as unplanned
downtime and triggers SLA credit as normal.

FORCE MAJEURE EXCLUSIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SLA credits are NOT applicable for downtime caused by:
  - Acts of God, natural disasters, government orders
  - Third-party power outages at client premises
  - Client-side equipment failure or misconfiguration
  - Upstream international transit outages beyond Silknet's AS border
  - DDoS attacks targeting the client directly

For disputes, escalate to legal@silknet.com with the incident Jira ticket number.
