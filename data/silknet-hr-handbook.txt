1. ABOUT SILKNET
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Silknet LLC is Georgia's leading telecommunications and internet service provider,
headquartered at 18 Tsereteli Avenue, Tbilisi 0112. We operate nationwide fiber,
mobile (4G/5G), and enterprise networking services for over 1.2 million subscribers.
Our workforce of approximately 3,400 employees is spread across Tbilisi (head office),
Kutaisi, Batumi, Rustavi, and regional technical hubs.

2. WORKING HOURS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Standard office hours are Monday through Friday, 09:00–18:00 (Tbilisi Time).
A one-hour unpaid lunch break is taken between 13:00 and 14:00.

Flexible start: Head-office employees in non-customer-facing roles may start between
08:00 and 10:00, provided they complete 8 working hours. Flexible start must be agreed
in writing with the direct manager.

Field technicians and call-centre staff follow shift schedules assigned by their
department head. Shift rosters are published at least two weeks in advance.

Overtime must be pre-approved in writing by the department head. Compensatory time off
is granted at a 1.5× rate for weekday overtime and 2× for weekend/public-holiday work.

3. REMOTE WORK (HYBRID) POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Eligibility: Employees who have completed their 3-month probation period and whose role
is classified as "desk-eligible" by HR may apply for hybrid working.

Entitlement: Eligible employees may work remotely up to two (2) days per week.
Remote days must be agreed with the line manager at the start of each week.

Mandatory in-office days: Tuesday and Thursday are company-wide anchor days — all
desk-eligible employees are expected to be in the office on these days unless on
approved leave or business travel.

Equipment: Silknet provides each hybrid employee with a laptop, VPN token, and mobile
data SIM (up to 20 GB/month on the Silknet Business plan at no cost). Employees are
responsible for a safe and ergonomic home workspace.

Connectivity: Employees must maintain a stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps
upload) during remote hours. Silknet's corporate VPN must be active for all internal
system access.

Performance: Remote employees are held to the same KPIs and availability standards as
in-office staff. Cameras must be on during team meetings unless prior agreement is
reached with the meeting organiser.

4. ANNUAL LEAVE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Entitlement: Every permanent employee accrues 24 working days of paid annual leave per
calendar year (per Georgian Labour Code, minimum is 20; Silknet provides 4 additional
days as a company benefit).

Accrual: Leave accrues at 2 days per month from the date of hire. New employees may
take accrued leave after completing 6 months of service.

Carry-over: Up to 5 unused leave days may be carried over to the following calendar
year. Any balance beyond 5 days is forfeited on 31 December unless HR approval is
obtained due to operational necessity.

Request process: Leave requests must be submitted through the Miriani HR portal at
least 5 working days in advance for periods of 1–5 days, and at least 15 working days
in advance for periods exceeding 5 days. The direct manager approves or rejects
requests within 2 working days.

Public holidays: Georgia's 16 public holidays are non-working days in addition to
annual leave. See the official list published by the Government of Georgia each year.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
5. SICK LEAVE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Employees are entitled to up to 15 calendar days of paid sick leave per year.
A medical certificate from a licensed physician is required for absences exceeding
2 consecutive working days. The certificate must be submitted to HR within 3 working
days of return.

For long-term illness exceeding 15 days, the employee transitions to the Georgian
Social Service Agency disability/sick-pay scheme; HR will assist with documentation.

6. MATERNITY & PATERNITY LEAVE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Maternity leave: 183 calendar days (of which 126 are paid at 100 % of average salary
by the State; Silknet tops up the remainder to 100 % for up to 60 additional days).
Employees may extend unpaid to 365 days total.

Paternity leave: 5 working days of paid leave within the first 4 weeks after birth.
Silknet additionally grants an optional 10 days of unpaid parental leave in the same
period.

Adoption: Same entitlements as maternity/paternity leave apply from the date of legal
adoption.

7. PERFORMANCE & PROBATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Probation period: All new hires serve a 3-month probationary period. During probation,
either party may terminate employment with 24 hours' written notice.

Performance reviews: Formal bi-annual reviews are held in June and December. Goals
(OKRs) are set at the start of each half-year with the line manager and stored in the
Silknet HR system. Rating scale: Exceeds Expectations / Meets Expectations /
Needs Improvement / Unsatisfactory.

Salary review: Annual salary adjustments are linked to the December performance review
and market benchmarking. Merit increases take effect from 1 March.

8. CODE OF CONDUCT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
All Silknet employees are expected to act with honesty, respect, and professionalism.
Harassment, discrimination, and bullying of any kind are grounds for immediate
disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Conflicts of interest must be declared in writing to the Compliance department before
any engagement that could compromise impartiality (e.g., working with a supplier,
owning shares in a competitor).

Confidential business information — customer data, network architecture, financial
results — must never be shared externally without written authorization from the
Legal department.

9. DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Step 1 — Verbal warning (documented in HR file).
Step 2 — Written warning.
Step 3 — Final written warning with a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
Step 4 — Termination with statutory notice period.

Gross misconduct (fraud, violence, data breach, etc.) may result in immediate
termination without prior steps, subject to Georgian Labour Code Article 37.
