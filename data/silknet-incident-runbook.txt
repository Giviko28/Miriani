INCIDENT SEVERITY LEVELS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

P1 — CRITICAL (Major Outage)
  Definition: Core backbone segment down; OR >1,000 residential subscribers affected;
              OR any Platinum/Enterprise client fully offline.
  Response target:  NOC engineer acknowledges within 5 minutes.
  Bridge call:      Mandatory within 15 minutes. Bridge: +995 32 222 6001 pin 9911.
  Escalation:       NOC Lead → Head of Network Operations → CTO (auto-paged via PagerDuty).
  Restoration:      15 min for Platinum; 30 min for Enterprise; 4 h for mass residential.
  Communications:   Status page updated immediately; TAM calls Platinum clients within
                    10 minutes; automated SMS to Enterprise contacts.

P2 — HIGH (Regional Degradation)
  Definition: Regional node degraded; 100–999 subscribers affected; OR SME client down;
              OR Enterprise client experiencing packet loss >5 % sustained.
  Response target:  NOC engineer acknowledges within 15 minutes.
  Bridge call:      If not resolved within 30 minutes, open bridge call.
  Escalation:       NOC Lead notified within 30 minutes; Head of Ops at 2 hours.
  Restoration:      2 hours for Enterprise; 4 hours for SME; 8 hours residential.
  Communications:   Status page updated; TAM emails affected Enterprise clients.

P3 — MEDIUM (Localised Outage)
  Definition: Single node or street cabinet down; <100 subscribers; no Enterprise client.
  Response target:  Assigned to field team within 1 hour.
  Escalation:       NOC Lead if unresolved after 4 hours.
  Restoration:      4 hours for SME; 8 hours residential.
  Communications:   Ticket update only; no proactive customer contact unless requested.

P4 — LOW (Single Subscriber / Degraded Service)
  Definition: One subscriber down or slow; no SLA breach.
  Response target:  Assigned to field team within 4 hours during business hours.
  Restoration:      Next business day acceptable.
  Communications:   Standard ticket update.

IMPORTANT — Corporate client rule: Always classify a corporate client incident ONE
level higher than the subscriber count would normally suggest. A single Platinum client
offline = P1 regardless of scale.

P1 RESPONSE PROCEDURE — STEP BY STEP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1 — Detect and confirm
  Check fiber monitoring (Zabbix dashboard: noc.silknet.ge/zabbix).
  Confirm alarm is not a false positive: cross-reference SNMP traps and customer calls.
  Identify affected segment: use the network topology map (Atlas → Backbone View).

Step 2 — Create Jira incident ticket immediately
  Project: NOC | Issue type: Incident | Priority: Critical
  Title format: "[P1] <Segment/Client> — <Symptom>" e.g. "[P1] Tbilisi-Gori Backbone — Fiber Cut"
  Required fields: affected segment, estimated subscriber count, first detected time,
  monitoring alarm IDs. Use Miriani to draft the Jira ticket: type the incident details
  and Miriani will format and create the P1 ticket for you.

Step 3 — Activate bridge call
  Dial +995 32 222 6001, enter pin 9911.
  Roles on the call: NOC Lead (chair), assigned engineer, TAM (if corporate client affected),
  field dispatch coordinator.

Step 4 — Diagnose
  Fiber cut: Check OTDR at both ends. Dispatch field crew to km marker shown on OTDR.
  Node failure: Check power (UPS status via BMS), check hardware (console port if accessible),
  attempt remote reboot via out-of-band management (OOB: oob.silknet.ge).
  BGP/routing issue: Check Huawei NCE / Cisco NSO for route withdrawals; check upstream
  peers (Cogent AS174, DE-CIX Frankfurt session) via looking glass.

Step 5 — Mitigate
  If fiber cut: reroute traffic via alternate path (pre-configured in Huawei NCE as
  "Emergency Path <segment>"). Notify field crew for physical repair.
  If node failure: failover to standby node if dual-homed. Otherwise escalate to vendor
  (Huawei TAC: +852 2872 2288; Cisco TAC: +1 800 553 2447).
  If BGP: contact upstream NOC; apply route filter or prepend as needed.

Step 6 — Notify clients
  Platinum: TAM calls primary technical contact within 10 minutes of P1 declaration.
  Enterprise: Automated email via NOC system; TAM follows up by phone.
  Use Miriani to draft client outage notification emails: "Draft outage email to [Client]
  about the [issue] affecting them since [time]."

Step 7 — Resolve and close
  Confirm service restored on monitoring. Wait 5 minutes for stability.
  Update Jira ticket: set status to Resolved, fill in RCA fields.
  Send restoration notification to all affected clients.
  Initiate post-incident review within 48 hours for all P1 events.
